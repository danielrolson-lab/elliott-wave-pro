Frequently Asked Questions

What data subscription do I need for real-time data?

Elliott Wave Pro uses Polygon.io for market data. For real-time WebSocket ticks, a Polygon Starter plan (free tier) covers US stocks. For options chain data (GEX, IV surface, flow feed), you need the Polygon Options add-on. For FINRA dark pool data, Polygon Business plan or higher is required.

Historical data (Wave Scanner, backfill) is available on any Polygon plan.

How does the Elliott Wave engine work?

The engine detects swing highs and lows using an adaptive ATR-based ZigZag algorithm. It generates all valid impulse and corrective wave count candidates and scores them using a Bayesian posterior that incorporates:

  • Fibonacci ratio adherence (wave lengths, retracements, extensions)
  • RSI-14 momentum alignment per wave
  • MACD histogram direction per wave
  • Volume profile (Wave 3 should have highest volume)
  • Multi-timeframe alignment with the 1H and 4H counts

The top 4 candidates are displayed as scenarios, ranked by posterior probability. The engine runs on every new candle close.

Why does the wave count change so often?

Elliott Wave counts are inherently ambiguous until a pattern completes. The 4-scenario panel is designed to show you all plausible interpretations simultaneously, not just one fixed count. As price develops and new pivots form, posterior probabilities update and scenarios may reorder or change.

This is normal and reflects the genuine uncertainty in real-time wave analysis. Use the probability bars to size your conviction accordingly.

How do I restore my subscription after reinstalling?

Go to Settings → Subscription and tap Restore Purchases. Sign in with the same Apple ID or Google account you used for the original purchase. Your subscription will be restored automatically via RevenueCat.

Can I use Elliott Wave Pro on multiple devices?

Yes. Sign in with the same account on each device. Your watchlist, alerts, and trade journal sync via Supabase. Your subscription entitlement is tied to your Apple ID or Google account and is available on all devices signed in with that account.

How do I cancel my subscription?

iOS: Open the App Store → tap your profile picture → Subscriptions → Elliott Wave Pro → Cancel.

Android: Open the Google Play Store → Menu → Subscriptions → Elliott Wave Pro → Cancel.

Your subscription remains active until the end of the current billing period. We do not offer refunds for partial billing periods, but Apple and Google may at their discretion.

What is the Quant API (Elite tier)?

The Quant API is a REST + WebSocket interface that exposes Elliott Wave Pro's engine output for algorithmic use:

  • GET /wave-count — primary wave count for any ticker
  • GET /scenarios — all 4 ranked scenarios
  • GET /regime — current market regime
  • GET /gex — GEX levels (Zero GEX, Call Wall, Put Wall)
  • GET /signals — composite wave + regime + GEX signal
  • WebSocket — real-time probability_change, count_flip, target_reached events

Rate limit: 50,000 requests/day on Elite. API key management is available in Settings.

The AI commentary stopped updating. What's wrong?

AI commentary triggers only when the primary scenario probability shifts by more than 5% and is debounced to avoid excessive requests. If the market is in a low-volatility period with stable wave counts, commentary may not update for extended periods — this is expected behaviour.

If commentary never appears, check that EXPO_PUBLIC_AI_COMMENTARY_URL is set correctly in your environment, and that the Vercel proxy is deployed with a valid ANTHROPIC_API_KEY.

Is Elliott Wave Pro suitable for beginners?

Elliott Wave Pro is designed for active traders who are already familiar with Elliott Wave Theory and technical analysis. The AI commentary is intended to help interpret wave counts in plain English, but understanding the underlying theory will significantly improve how you use the app.

The app does not provide investment advice. All wave counts are probabilistic scenarios, not trade recommendations.
